Timeline: Celebrating Women's Achievements

Check out how women have broken barriers and made history across various fields throughout the years.

⚇

The Seneca Falls Convention

1848

The Seneca Falls Convention was a pivotal event where women and men gathered to advocate for women's rights, including suffrage, and it set the stage for further advancements in gender equality.

⚇

Marie Curie becomes the first woman to win a Nobel Prize

1903

Marie Curie's groundbreaking contributions to science, particularly her research on radioactivity, led to her becoming the first woman to win a Nobel Prize, marking a significant achievement for women in the field of scientific discovery.

⚇

The 19th Amendment to the US Constitution grants women the right to vote

1920

The 19th Amendment to the US Constitution, ratified in 1920, granted women the fundamental right to vote, marking a historic achievement in the struggle for gender equality and political empowerment.

⚇

Amelia Earhart becomes the first woman to fly solo across the Pacific Ocean

1937

Amelia Earhart's historic solo flight across the Pacific Ocean made her the first woman to achieve this feat, breaking barriers in aviation and inspiring future generations of female pilots and adventurers.

⚇

Rosa Parks refuses to give up her seat on a bus

1955

Rosa Parks' refusal to give up her seat on a bus in Montgomery, Alabama, sparked the Montgomery Bus Boycott and became a pivotal moment in the civil rights movement, highlighting the ongoing struggle against racial segregation and discrimination.

⚇

Valentina Tereshkova becomes the first woman to travel to space.

1963

Valentina Tereshkova's historic journey as the first woman in space marked a significant milestone in space exploration, showcasing women's capabilities in astronautics and paving the way for future female astronauts.

⚇

Wangari Maathai: First African woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize

2004

Wangari Maathai's recognition as the first African woman to receive the Nobel Peace Prize honored her outstanding contributions to sustainable development, environmental conservation, democracy, and peacebuilding in Kenya and beyond.

⚇

Malala Yousafzai survives an assassination attempt and becomes a global advocate for girls' education

2012

Malala Yousafzai's survival of an assassination attempt by the Taliban not only demonstrated her resilience but also catapulted her into a global spotlight as a passionate advocate for girls' education, inspiring countless individuals to champion educational rights worldwide.

⚇

Kamala Harris makes history as the first female Vice President of the United States

2021

Kamala Harris's historic achievement as the first female Vice President of the United States shattered barriers and symbolized a significant step forward in promoting diversity, inclusion, and gender equality in the highest levels of government.
